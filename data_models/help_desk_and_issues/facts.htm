Subject: Table Design and Relationships
Question: I was looking at your data model for Help Desk and was wondering if I might ask your advice on how best to proceed with my project. 
I am tasked with building a similar DB for my business. 

The trouble I am having is how to build the relationships and table model with not just 1 "incident type" but 3 incident types. 

These 3 different incident types are referred to as Incidents, Problems, Changes. 

These 3 have very little in common in the way of similar fields but they can also be related to each other or not at all. 

Example: I user calls with an issue. 
We will then open the Incident ticket and fix the issue. 
If the issue is reoccurring we may generate more incidents which will be children of this first issue and we might even open a 
Problem ticket for 2nd or 3rd lvl support. 

Problem tickets cannot exist without an Incident ticket or tickets attached. 

A Change record would then be created if this Problem required an update to the application itself. 

So you see how the 3 can be related. 

Incidents are created for most issues. 

Problems are created from Incidents (no exceptions) 
Changes can be the result of a Problem or multiple problems ... or it can simply be a routine update 
to a system and will have no Problems or Incidents attached. 

This is my issue. 

If I ere to use your Help Desk Model can you advise how I might include 2 other tables and then how to relate them? 

Any suggestions would be greatly beneficial. 

If you require any further information please let me know and I will be happy to provide it. 

Thank you for your time.
